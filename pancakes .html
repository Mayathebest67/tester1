So… What Exactly Are Pancakes?

Pancakes are one of the oldest and most loved breakfast foods in the world. They are thin, round cakes made from a batter of flour, eggs, milk, and butter — cooked on a hot griddle until golden and fluffy.

At The Original Pancake House, pancakes are more than breakfast… they’re a tradition. Every recipe is made with real ingredients, fresh batter, and techniques passed down for generations.

A Short History of Pancakes

Pancakes have been around for thousands of years — even ancient civilizations cooked early versions of them!

Over time, different cultures created their own styles, from thin crepes to thick fluffy stacks. Today, pancakes are enjoyed everywhere, and OPH continues the tradition with recipes perfected since 1953.

How Pancakes Are Made

1. Mix the Batter

Flour, eggs, milk, and butter are blended until smooth.

2. Heat the Griddle

A hot, lightly buttered surface gives pancakes their golden color.

3. Pour & Flip

The batter is poured, bubbles form, and then — the perfect flip.

4. Serve Warm

With syrup, fruit, whipped cream, or OPH’s famous specialty toppings.

Pancakes Around the World

🇫🇷 France — Crêpes

Thin, delicate pancakes often filled with fruit or chocolate.

🇩🇪 Germany — Dutch Baby

A fluffy oven‑baked pancake served with lemon and powdered sugar.

🇸🇪 Sweden — Swedish Pancakes

Soft, thin pancakes served with lingonberries.

🇺🇸 United States — Buttermilk Pancakes

Thick, fluffy, and served in stacks — a true breakfast classic.
